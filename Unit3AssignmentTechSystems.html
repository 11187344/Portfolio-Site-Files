In Unit 2 Technology Systems, we’ve been learning about how different parts of computer systems work together and how they’re used in everyday life. The unit covers things like input and output devices, storage, software, networks, and how information flows through systems.
One of the things I found interesting was learning about the different parts inside a computer, like the CPU, RAM, motherboard, power supply, and storage devices. We learned what each part does and how they all work together to keep the system running properly. It was also useful to understand how these components affect the performance of a computer and what to look for when building or upgrading a PC.
This unit has helped me understand more about how technology fits into different areas of work and life. It’s made me more confident in talking about IT systems and how they operate. I can now recognize different parts of a system and explain how they all work together.
